The Toronto Streets Guessing Game!

I've had this idea for a while now, but I finally thought the time had come to test it out. A few guidelines:

You need the full name, unless the street is one of these types: Acres, Alley, Avenue, Boulevard, Byway, Circuit, Close, Court, Crescent, Drive, Expressway, Gardens, Gate, Grove, Heights, Hill, Keep, Lane, Laneway, Lawn, Manor, Mews, Overpass, Park, Parkway, Path, Pathway, Place, Plaza, Point, Quay, Ridge, Road, Roadway, Square, Street, Terrace, Trail, Walk, Walkway, Way, Wharf, and Wood.

A note about "Way": a bunch of private streets in North York are sometimes labeled "Way" with a prefix. Most of these flow well if you split the prefix, so you have to guess the street name and the prefix for these! As a goodwill gesture you only need the street names for "-ways" with these types: Carseway, Shepway, and Starway.

Oh, one last thing: all numbered streets are written with letters and no hyphens, because that's how the City does it. Have fun!
